How This Works

Yes, someone actually built this for you
🌴What Is This Thing
This is your group's personal Key West command center. Eight adults. Four nights. One app. What could go wrong.

It has your flights, the weather, a shared calendar, local recommendations, a photo gallery, and a button that finds the nearest bar. It also has a button that sends Chris home. We'll get to that.
👤Who Are You
The first time you open the app you'll see a screen asking who you are. Tap your drink emoji. The app remembers you forever after that — your name shows up in the greeting, your check-ins are tagged to you, and your Do Not Push count is yours.

If you picked the wrong person — it happens, especially after a few drinks — tap Not you? under your name on the home screen and pick again.

Your drink emoji: Christy 🍷 · Bill 🥃 · Leighann 🧉 · Mike 🍻 · Lisa 🍺 · Chris 🫗 · Rachel 🍹 · Andrew 🧃
📲Add This To Your Home Screen
Do this now. Before you forget. Before the rum.

On iPhone (Safari only — won't work in Chrome):
1. Make sure you're viewing this in Safari. If you opened it in Chrome, copy the link and paste it into Safari.
2. Tap the Share button at the bottom of the screen — it looks like a box with an arrow pointing up ⬆️
3. Scroll down in the menu that pops up until you see "Add to Home Screen"
4. Tap it. Give it a name if you want. Tap Add in the top right corner.
5. Done. It now lives on your home screen like a real app.

On Android (Andrew):
1. Tap the three dots ⋮ in the top right corner of Chrome
2. Tap "Add to Home screen" or "Install app"
3. Tap Add. Done.
4. If it doesn't work, that's on Android, not us.

If you can't figure it out, ask Andrew. He brought a drone to a beach vacation, he can handle this.
📅The Calendar
All trip events live here — group events and couple events. Tap any event to get walking directions from wherever you're standing right now.

Tap the ✏️ icon on any event to edit the details or delete it entirely. Tap + Add Event at the bottom to add something new.

The home screen shows today's schedule automatically — no need to dig into the calendar just to know what's happening.
📍Where Is Everyone
When you go somewhere, tap Check In My Location and enter where you are. Takes five seconds.

Each person has a 💬 text and 📞 call button next to their name — tap either to reach them directly without leaving the app.

When someone checks in, everyone in the group gets an automatic text notification. No need to be watching the app — it comes to you.

⚠️ Important: Checking in at Sloppy Joe's at 4pm and then going dark does not mean you are still at Sloppy Joe's at midnight. It means you were at Sloppy Joe's at 4pm. Nobody knows where you are now. You could be at The Roost. You could be at The Saint. You could be face down in an alley off Duval Street serenading a stray cat.

Chris. We are looking at you. Update your check-in. Lisa found you once. She divorced you once too. Don't push it.
🆘I Need...
Your lifeline tab. Eight buttons covering every vacation emergency:

🍺 I'm Thirsty — nearest 3 bars with directions
🍔 I'm Hungry — nearest 3 restaurants
☕ Need Coffee — nearest 3 cafes
🏖️ Beach Me — nearest beach access
🛍️ I Want to Shop — nearest shops
🛏️ I Need Sleep — directions back to The Reach. No thinking required. Designed for use after midnight on Duval Street when thinking is no longer an option.
🍋 I Need Key Lime Pie — the definitive guide to 9 spots ranked by local consensus
🦪 I Need Oysters — 6 spots ranked by Reddit, with honest notes

🚨 I Need Help — genuine emergencies only. Hospital, pharmacy, 911, hotel phone. We hope you never need it. We're also realists about four nights on Duval Street.
💡Local Recs
Over 50 curated spots across bars, breakfast, dinner, beaches, attractions and landmarks. Ratings pulled live from Google.

The 💬 teal notes are real local intel — we scraped Reddit's r/KeyWest and other social media so you get the unfiltered opinions of people who actually live there and visit regularly, not sponsored TripAdvisor content. When a local says Half Shell Raw Bar is the best oyster spot on the island, that means something. When they warn you about shell shards at Alonzo's, that also means something.

Use the search bar at the top to find anything instantly. Type "oysters," "breakfast," "cheap," "meringue," or "local" and matching spots appear immediately. No scrolling required.

Every listing is tappable for walking directions.
📸Photos
Take photos with your regular camera app like a normal person. Then come here and tap Upload Photos to share your favorites with the group.

Or tap Take Photo to shoot directly in the app. Quality is slightly lower but perfectly fine for "proof that Bill actually caught a fish" and "evidence of whatever Chris was doing at 1am."

At the end of the trip everyone has access to everyone else's photos. No more group texts with 47 attachments that half the group never downloads.

If you upload something you want to remove, tap the ✕ button on your own photo thumbnail to delete it. You can only delete photos you uploaded — everyone else's are safe from you.
✈️Flights
All four United flight legs are in here — SDF to ORD, ORD to EYW outbound on July 7th, and both legs home on July 11th.

The app is connected to live flight data and will automatically update flight status as the airline makes changes — delays, gate changes, cancellations. You don't need to do anything.

Important: This feature won't show accurate live data until approximately 2 weeks before departure — around June 23rd. Before that the status badges will be blank. That's normal. Once the flights appear in the system they'll update automatically.

Tap Live Flight Status to check United's site directly. Tap My Booking to manage your own reservation — your confirmation number is your business.
🗺️Directions
Almost everything in this app is tappable for directions. Calendar events, local recs, landmarks, I Need results — tap it and Google Maps opens with walking directions from wherever you're standing.

Key West is about two miles end to end. You are almost certainly closer than you think. Unless you are Chris, in which case your exact position is unknown and has been for the last three hours.
🚁A Note About the Drone
Andrew has a drone. Possibly two. Nobody is surprised.

Key West has FAA flight restrictions over parts of the island and near the airport. Before Andrew launches anything over Duval Street at sunset, someone should probably mention this. That someone is not going to be Chris, who will be busy.

On the bright side, if Chris does go missing, Andrew's drone has a better chance of finding him than any of us do on foot. Every group needs a drone guy. We've got ours.

📸 Aerial photos from Andrew's drone are welcome in the shared photo gallery.
🏖️Final Thought
This app exists so that eight adults who theoretically know how to travel can spend less time texting "where are you" and more time actually being in Key West together.

Use it. Check in when you wander off. Share your photos. Tap the sleep button when the time comes. Let Andrew fly his drone. Keep an eye on Chris.

Now put your phone down and go enjoy the island. 🌴
🤖A Note About Andrew
Andrew uses an Android.

The app works on Android. Mostly. We think. We haven't tested it as thoroughly as the seven iPhone users in the group because, frankly, his opinion counts slightly less in these matters.

If Andrew reports a bug, we will log it, consider it carefully, and then ask one of the iPhone users if they can reproduce it before doing anything about it.

Andrew, if you're reading this: we appreciate you. We appreciate your drone. We do not appreciate your life choices in mobile operating systems. Welcome aboard anyway. 🤖
